The UN goals 3 and 6 are linked through waterborne diseases. These diseases make you sick, like the flu. Waterborne diseases spread when people drink, or use water that has been contamiated by human waste, or "poop".

Cholera is an example of a waterborne disease. It causes severe diarrhea and vomiting, which can then lead to dehydration (when your body doesn't have enough water) and death. This diarrhea can then spread to other water sources, contaminating them. Because of this, cholera can easily spread in areas with poor sanitation and harm many people.
